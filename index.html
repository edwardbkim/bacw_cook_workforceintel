Key Findings
Housing · Critical

Homeownership is structurally unaffordable in two of three markets

Bloomington (-$8.31/hr) and Winston-Salem (-$7.98/hr) production workers cannot afford to buy a home on a single income at the local production median wage. This is not a wage problem; it is a structural retention risk. Workers who cannot build equity are workers who leave. Canton is the only Cook market where the math works: +$0.80/hr over the homeownership threshold on a $107,100 median home value.

Bloomington · Opportunity

Novo Nordisk's exit creates a rare talent injection

Approximately 400 skilled biomanufacturing workers will enter the Bloomington labor market in May 2026. The Assembler LQ of 3.97 means Bloomington already has the deepest concentration of this exact worker profile in the country. Cook should move immediately. Simtra BioPharma, which is expanding in the same market, will be absorbing from the same pool.

Winston-Salem · Scale

Winston-Salem is Cook's most scalable long-term recruiting market

22,950 production workers, +7.4% population growth, 11.3% mobility rate (vs. 21.8% in Bloomington). The challenge here is not access; it is differentiation. Reynolds American (2,500 employees) sets the effective wage floor in Forsyth County. Cook must benchmark against RAI, not just OEWS medians, and compete on career pathway, stability, and brand.

Canton · Retention

Canton's affordability edge should anchor the retention strategy

Canton workers can afford a home at median production wages, something no Cook worker in Bloomington or Winston-Salem can do on a single income. This is a concrete, communicable retention differentiator. The risk is structural: the population has declined 11.5% since 2015. Cook's Canton strategy must prioritize tenure over volume. Caterpillar's UAW wage history shapes expectations; Cook competes on stability and medical device prestige.

Competitive Intelligence
Who Cook Actually Competes Against
Key Competitive Insight
Cook's three markets face three fundamentally different competitive regimes, and the wrong benchmark in each one costs real headcount.
BloomingtonTalent compression. Simtra's $241M expansion (+130 jobs) collides with Novo Nordisk's approximately 400 May 2026 layoffs, the same specialist pool, simultaneously pulled and released.
Winston-SalemWage-floor regime. Reynolds American (BAT), not OEWS medians, sets the effective wage floor. Cook offers priced at $19.08/hr lose before the interview.
CantonCyclical anchor. Caterpillar's UAW wages ($28 to 32/hr) define the ceiling. Cook competes on stability, not wage, and on homeownership, not growth.

Bloomington, IN · Monroe + Owen Counties
Life Sciences Cluster: Concentrated, Competitive, and Active
Bloomington is the #1 small MSA for medical device employment concentration and #2 for biopharma. Cook competes inside a deep but narrow specialist pool.
These are not generic production workers. They are trained biomanufacturing professionals: sterile fill/finish operators, QA analysts, validation specialists, and maintenance mechanics. Their skills map directly onto medical device manufacturing. Cook should stand up a targeted outreach campaign before Simtra, Boston Scientific, and other area employers absorb this pool.
What This Means for Cook
Recruitment window; act now. Stand up targeted outreach via LinkedIn InMail campaigns, local job fair presence, and direct referral incentives. Prioritize QA analysts, production technicians, and maintenance mechanics.
Retention Signal
Novo's regulatory troubles (FDA warning letter, Jul 2025) and global restructuring signal pharma industry volatility. Cook's medical device stability is a credible counternarrative for workers who want out of the pharma cycle.
SourcesXtalks / BioSpace: Novo Bloomington layoffs (Apr 2026) · Indiana DWD WARN Act filings · FDA Warning Letters
Simtra is Cook's most acute ongoing competitor in Bloomington. They are growing, not contracting. Their production specialist and QA analyst postings run $22 to $38/hr, above Cook's likely range. 33 active roles on Indeed confirm persistent hiring activity from the same labor pool Cook draws from.
What This Means for Cook
Simtra's expansion directly compresses the Bloomington production labor supply at exactly the moment Novo's layoffs temporarily expand it. Cook needs to move quickly on Novo's displaced workers; Simtra will be recruiting the same people simultaneously.
SourcesIndiana Daily Student: Simtra expansion (Aug 2025) · Indiana Economic Development Corp.
Boston Scientific's Spencer, IN facility draws from the same Monroe/Owen County labor shed as Cook Bloomington. Their 13 active Indiana postings confirm persistent recruiting activity. As a medical device peer, BSC is a natural destination for Cook workers exploring alternatives.
Retention Risk
Career mobility risk, especially for workers who value medical device prestige and want a larger corporate environment. BSC's brand recognition may attract mid-tenure Cook employees who feel they have capped advancement potential internally.
SourcesBoston Scientific careers · BSC 10-K (SEC EDGAR) · Indeed employer listings, Feb to Apr 2026
Consistent presence in Bloomington production and operator searches. Competes for entry-level production workers in the $18 to $24/hr band, overlapping with Cook's Assembler range.
Recruitment Implication
Low strategic threat but contributes to entry-level wage floor pressure. Candidates choosing between Cook and Printpack are likely new-to-market production workers where offer speed and onboarding quality matter most. Cook's mission advantage should be the differentiator.
SourcesPrintpack facility network · Indeed Bloomington postings, Feb to Apr 2026
Winston-Salem, NC · 5-County MSA
Industrial Heritage Market: Fragmented, Differentiation-Driven
Winston-Salem's blue-collar labor market is shaped by legacy manufacturers (tobacco, apparel) and growing precision manufacturing. Reynolds American sets the effective wage floor.
Reynolds American is the single largest manufacturing employer in Forsyth County. As a BAT subsidiary, it offers compensation and benefits that set the practical wage ceiling for blue-collar production workers in the Winston-Salem MSA.
What This Means for Cook
Critical benchmark: Cook's wage strategy in Winston-Salem must be benchmarked against Reynolds American, not just OEWS medians. The $20.08/hr production median understates what experienced production workers expect based on what they see Reynolds paying their peers.
SourcesGreater Winston-Salem, Inc.: Major Employers · BAT PLC Annual Report · WStoday: top WS employers (2025)
Hanesbrands was acquired by Gildan (Dec 2025). Post-acquisition workforce restructuring is likely as Gildan consolidates operations. Assembly, packaging, and sewing workers at Hanesbrands have directly relevant dexterous manufacturing skills for medical device work.
Emerging Opportunity
If Gildan restructures local operations, Cook has an opportunity to recruit dislodged Hanesbrands assembly and packaging workers. These workers have stable employment histories and precision hand-assembly experience. Monitor closely through mid-2026. Any Gildan restructuring announcement is a recruitment trigger.
SourcesGildan: acquisition press release (2024) · HanesBrands 8-K (SEC EDGAR) · Greater Winston-Salem employer list
Collins Aerospace (RTX subsidiary) competes for the same skilled production workers Cook needs most: machinists, QA inspectors, maintenance mechanics, and precision operators.
Why Posted Wages Are Above OEWS Median
Collins Aerospace is the likely explanation for Winston-Salem's assembler posted range of $22 to $24/hr vs. the OEWS median of $19.08/hr. Cook should set pay offers at or above the posted range, not at the OEWS benchmark. Pricing at OEWS loses skilled candidates to Collins before the offer stage.
SourcesRTX newsroom · RTX 10-K (SEC EDGAR) · Greater Winston-Salem employer list
Important clarification: Lilly is not a direct Winston-Salem MSA competitor for hourly production workers. However, Lilly's $70k+ average pay has reshaped statewide pharma manufacturing wage expectations across North Carolina.
What This Means for Cook
NC production workers increasingly compare themselves to statewide pharma benchmarks, not just local wages. This perception gap should be addressed in compensation communications. When workers say "Lilly pays more," Cook's response should acknowledge the regional context while explaining the medical device career advantage.
SourcesEli Lilly investor news: Concord NC expansion (2023) · BLS OEWS: NC pharma manufacturing
High posting volume in Winston-Salem production and forklift searches. Largest volume competitor for general production and warehouse labor in the market.
Recruitment Implication
Cook's differentiation against Smurfit Westrock should be the medical device career track and Cook's mission-driven positioning. Entry-level candidates choosing between Cook and Smurfit are evaluating stability versus volume; Cook wins on long-term trajectory and purpose of work.
SourcesSmurfit Westrock: merger close (Jul 2024) · Indeed Winston-Salem postings, Feb to Apr 2026
Peoria / Canton, IL · Fulton County
Caterpillar Country: Wage Expectations Set by UAW, Pool Defined by CAT Cycles
Caterpillar's East Peoria complex defines what blue-collar manufacturing wages look like in this MSA. Cook competes against a UAW-calibrated wage expectation that exceeds its likely pay range.
Caterpillar's East Peoria and Mossville operations define the Peoria labor market. UAW-represented production workers earn $25 to $45/hr with industry-leading benefits. The CAT brand and wage scale shapes what every production worker in the region considers baseline manufacturing compensation.
Recruitment Opportunity (Cyclical)
CAT layoff cycles periodically release highly skilled machinists, operators, and maintenance workers. Cook should focus on workers who want off the CAT cycle permanently, not those waiting for a recall. The screening question: "Are you looking for stable non-cyclical work?" filters for the right candidates.
Retention Risk
Any Cook Canton worker with prior CAT experience is a CAT recall risk. Medical device mission, stability narrative, and non-cyclical employment are the key differentiators. When CAT is actively hiring, Cook should expect elevated retention pressure from workers who view Cook as a bridge back to CAT wages.
SourcesCaterpillar newsroom · Caterpillar 10-K (SEC EDGAR) · UAW Local 974: CAT bargaining unit · Fulton County IL workforce statistics
ITW's diversified Peoria-area operations produce industrial and specialty products with precision manufacturing requirements similar to medical device work.
What This Means for Cook
Mid-tier threat for skilled and experienced workers. ITW is a stable non-cyclical employer, the same value proposition Cook offers, which makes it a closer peer competitor than Caterpillar. Workers who have already decided they do not want to return to CAT may compare Cook directly against ITW on compensation and advancement.
SourcesIllinois Tool Works 10-K (SEC EDGAR) · ITW newsroom · Indeed Peoria listings, Feb to Apr 2026
Graham Health System is Canton's largest employer with more than 4x Cook's local headcount (approximately 185 employees). As the dominant Canton institution, Graham has deep community ties and brand recognition that Cook cannot match within the city.
What This Means for Cook
For Canton-rooted workers, Graham is the most likely alternative if they leave Cook but want to stay local. Cook's retention strategy in Canton must emphasize manufacturing career advancement, the mission of medical device work, and the homeownership wage gap story. Workers who choose Cook over Graham are often prioritizing the manufacturing identity and production-level wages over service-sector stability.
SourcesFulton County IL: largest employers · Graham Health System
Maui Jim's Peoria optical manufacturing facility requires the same precision inspection and fine-motor dexterity as medical device QA work. The consumer brand association and lower-regulatory-burden environment are potential draws for Cook QA personnel.
Retention Risk
Niche but real risk for Cook's QA workforce specifically. Maui Jim's consumer brand and lower-pressure quality environment may appeal to workers experiencing audit fatigue or regulatory burnout in the medical device context. Cook should monitor Maui Jim posting activity as a leading indicator of QA retention pressure.
SourcesMaui Jim careers: Peoria facility · Maui Jim: company history · Indeed Peoria optical postings, Feb to Apr 2026
Recommended Workforce Strategy
One position per market. No hedging.
The scorecard produces three distinct decisions. Each market requires a different posture, not a different volume of the same playbook.
Directive 01 · Retain
Bloomington, IN

Shift from hiring to retention.

Invest in wage competitiveness and internal mobility. Simtra is expanding into the same specialist pool, mortgage affordability is structurally negative, and mobility is already high (21.8%). Every Cook worker lost here is replaced at Simtra's wage floor, not Cook's.

Stabilize and Retain
Directive 02 · Expand
Winston-Salem, NC

Primary expansion candidate.

Leverage available labor with targeted recruitment pipelines. 22,950 production workers, only 12 active assembler postings, +7.4% population growth. The constraint here is Reynolds American's wage floor, not supply. Price at posted range, not OEWS median, and capacity follows.

Expand Selectively
Directive 03 · Preserve
Canton / Peoria, IL

Avoid major expansion. Maintain with cost discipline.

The only positive mortgage gap in Cook's US footprint is a genuine retention asset, but the 11.5% population decline means this labor pool will not replenish. Preserve operations, lead with the homeownership story, and resist treating Canton as a growth market simply because costs are lower.

Monitor / Preserve
Recommendations for the CFO
Six moves, ranked by time-to-act.
Numbered in order of urgency. The Bloomington recommendations compress into weeks; Canton retention is multi-year. Each is framed to answer: what would a CFO need to greenlight this?
01
Bloomington · Immediate

Activate a Novo Nordisk recruitment campaign now

Approximately 400 skilled biomanufacturing workers enter the market in May 2026. Simtra BioPharma is expanding and will recruit from the same pool. Cook has a narrow window. Stand up targeted outreach: LinkedIn InMail campaigns, local job fair presence, direct referral incentives. Prioritize QA analysts, production technicians, and maintenance mechanics.

Time to act: weeks, not months
02
Bloomington · Retention

Address the -$8.31/hr mortgage gap with targeted housing assistance

Bloomington city workers cannot buy a home at the median production wage. Ellettsville and Spencer have materially better affordability (+$4.98/hr and +$6.98/hr rent gaps respectively). Consider housing assistance programs, down payment support, or employer-assisted mortgage initiatives.

Frame as retention ROI, not a benefit cost
03
Winston-Salem · Wages

Benchmark against Reynolds American, not OEWS medians

Reynolds American (2,500 Forsyth Co. employees, BAT benefits) sets the effective wage floor. Cook's offers priced at OEWS median ($19.08/hr assembler) will consistently lose candidates. The assembler posted range is already $22 to $24/hr, $3 to $5 above OEWS. Price offers at or above the posted market.

Compensate at posted range, not OEWS benchmark
04
Winston-Salem · Opportunity

Monitor Hanesbrands/Gildan for displacement events

Gildan's December 2025 acquisition of Hanesbrands (2,400 WS employees) creates workforce uncertainty. Post-acquisition restructuring is common. Hanesbrands assembly and packaging workers have directly transferable dexterous manufacturing skills. Establish a monitoring cadence; any Gildan restructuring announcement is a recruitment trigger.

Set up monitoring for Hanesbrands layoff news
05
Canton · Retention

Lead with the homeownership story; it is Cook's most unique retention asset

Canton is the only Cook US location where production wages clear the homeownership threshold. No competitor in any of Cook's markets can make this claim at comparable wages. This should be the centerpiece of Canton retention materials, recruitment ads, and manager talking points.

Deploy in job postings, offer letters, and onboarding
06
Canton · Long-Horizon

Build for retention depth, not hiring volume; the pool is contracting

Canton's population has declined 11.5% since 2015. The labor pool will not replenish. Any Cook worker who leaves Canton is harder to replace than five years ago. Prioritize CAT workers who have permanently left the CAT cycle, not those between recall cycles.

Identify "permanently off the CAT cycle" in screening
